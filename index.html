Finally Graduated, What Now?

Back at school days, the struggle was all about passing the years and graduating with hopefully good grades. The goal was clear and shared among all students. After graduating from univerisity, you will find yourself in a situation you have not been through before. There is no clear tomorrow and you will need to take full control on how you will spend the rest of your life. Do you want to pursue your studies? Do you need sometime off? Do you need to find a job and start receiving an annual income?

Many Many options will be waiting for you to try and explore. A great way to go through this is to ask and seek advise from others and learn from their experiences.

Stackoverflow sends a survey annually to their website’s users from all around the world and ask them 154 quesions to understand multiple aspects of jobs related to software development and data analytics such as salary, job satisfaction, most used languages and technologies, motivations to accept or quit jobs.. etc. I personally just started my career as a web developer/data scientist and I’m interested heavily to hear what the data say about the industry. Therefore, I used the Stackoverflow survey resutls from 2017 to answer some of the questions I have.

My third and last question: What are the strongest motivations behind people leaving their coding job?

The paricipants who left a coding job were asked 8 questions about the motivations behind their action:

You said before that you used to code as part of your job, but no longer do. To what extent do you agree or disagree with the following statements?
• If money weren't an issue, I would take a coding job again
• Working as a developer just wasn't for me
• I have better work-life balance now than I did as a developer
• My career is going the way I thought it would 10 years ago
• When I was a developer, I didn't feel like I belonged with my colleagues
• I don't think my coding skills are up to date
• I probably won't code for a living ever again
• I'm still active in the developer community

It seems like those who left coding jobs still enjoy coding and they still do it but they don't believe it's a secure job as it doesn't pay enough. In addition, coders spend so much time at work which highly affect the work-life balance that everyone should aim to maintain.
